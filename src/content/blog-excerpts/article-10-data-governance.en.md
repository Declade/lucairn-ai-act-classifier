# Article 10 — data and data governance

Article 10 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 applies to every high-risk AI system that "makes use of techniques involving the training of AI models with data" ([EUR-Lex Art 10(1)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=OJ:L_202401689)). The article does **not** narrow to specific Annex III paragraphs — if a system is classified as high-risk under Article 6 and is not prohibited under Article 5, Article 10 applies.

The substantive obligations land in paragraphs 2 through 5: design choices on data collection, data-preparation processes (annotation, labelling, cleaning, aggregation), examination for biases that may negatively affect protected groups, and statistical properties such as representativeness and relevance. Paragraph 3 lists the data-quality criteria; paragraph 4 covers context-specific characteristics; paragraph 5 permits the limited processing of special-category personal data for bias-correction purposes under strict safeguards.

The EU AI Office's [service-desk page](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/10/) clarifies that an Article 10 compliance file is a complement to, not a substitute for, GDPR Article 5 lawfulness-of-processing obligations. The [BfDI](https://www.bfdi.bund.de/) has been clear that GDPR continues to apply alongside the AI Act.
