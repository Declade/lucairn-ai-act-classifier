# Article 50 — the four transparency obligations for deployers and providers

Article 50 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 carries four independent transparency duties that do **not** depend on a system being classified as high-risk under Article 6 ([EUR-Lex Art 50](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=OJ:L_202401689)):

- **¶1 — interaction with natural persons.** Providers must inform exposed users they are interacting with an AI system, unless that fact is obvious from context.
- **¶2 — synthetic content marking.** Providers of AI systems (including GPAI) generating synthetic audio, image, video, or text must mark outputs as machine-readable and detectable as artificially generated.
- **¶3 — emotion recognition / biometric categorisation.** Deployers must inform exposed natural persons of the operation of the system and comply with the GDPR.
- **¶4 — deep fakes and public-interest text.** Deployers must disclose when image/audio/video content constitutes a deep fake; deployers must disclose when text published to inform the public on public-interest matters has been AI-generated.

Each paragraph has its own statutory carve-outs — law-enforcement use, artistic works, editorial review. The [Future of Life Institute regulation-text mirror (Tier-3)](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/) walks through them verbatim. Paragraph 5 sets the format-and-timing rule: information at the first interaction, clear and distinguishable, accessibility-compliant.
