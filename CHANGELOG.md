### Added

- **Day 5 — Article 12 + Article 50 rule modules + scripts/sync-three-category.ts + Lucairn three-category overlay.** Four new deliverables: (a) `src/rules/article-12.ts` (record-keeping cascade) mirroring the Day-4 high-risk-cascade shape (consumes `AnnexIIIResult + Article5Result`, emits `Article12Result` with `applicable`, `triggered_by`, EUR-Lex-cited summaries, source URL); (b) `src/rules/article-50.ts` (GPAI / deployer transparency) as a NEW non-cascade root NOT gated on Annex III high-risk and NOT suppressed by Article 5 (per 50(6) "without prejudice to other transparency obligations") — classifies FIVE paragraph paths independently (50(1) chatbot interaction, 50(2) synthetic content, 50(3) emotion-recognition or biometric-categorisation deployment, 50(4) sub-paragraph 1 deep fake content, 50(4) sub-paragraph 2 public-interest text), consuming the Day-2 lexicon's `article_50_gpai` group (Day-2 lexicon extended in this commit with a new `5_public_interest_text` category covering EUR-Lex Art 50(4) second sub-paragraph). Optional `annex` parameter wires a 50(3) fallback when Annex III paragraph 1 fires with sub-letter `b` (biometric categorisation) or `c` (emotion recognition) so Annex-III-style descriptions still surface the transparency obligation; (c) `scripts/sync-three-category.ts` build-time sync script with `--check` drift-detection mode, that reads the website's compliance checklist source-of-truth at `theveil-website/src/lib/compliance/checklist-content.ts` and emits `src/data/three-category.gen.json` (committed for reproducibility — the build script's `cp -R src/data dist/data` step ships the generated JSON in the npm tarball). CI gate: `pnpm sync:three-category:check` exits 1 on drift. (d) `src/rules/three-category.ts` Lucairn opinionated overlay projecting Day-4 cascade results (Art 10 / 12 / 14 / 15) into Cat 1 (Art 10+15 sanitizer) / Cat 2 (Art 12+14 evidence) / Cat 3 (Art 10+12+14+15 inventory) — strict-AND applicability: a category is `applicable: true` iff ALL its required articles are applicable. `triggered_articles` surfaces the partial-applicable subset for transparency. Article 13 and Article 50 are INTENTIONALLY OUT of any three-category pairing (Art 13 is transparency-to-deployer track; Art 50 has a different cascade root). Article 5 suppression propagates through Day-4 cascade modules to three-category automatically (no explicit article5 branch inside the overlay); a sanity guard throws on inconsistent upstream state (article5.prohibited true but annex.suppressed_by_article_5 false). `source` field is an OBJECT `{generated_file, source_file, version}` providing provenance metadata (NOT EUR-Lex — regulatory citations live on per-article modules). EUR-Lex EN+DE wording sourced via Tier-2 EU AI Office Service Desk (`/en/ai-act/article-{12,50}` + `/de/ai-act/article-{12,50}`); Tier-1 EUR-Lex HTML returned empty on programmatic fetch as of 2026-05-15 — Tier-1 PDF remains the canonical primary source. **Tier-3 cross-validation flags** (Day-3 lesson 5): Article 12 SUMMARY_DE uses Tier-3 artificialintelligenceact.eu/de/article/12/ verbatim because Tier-2 returned an abridged variant; Article 50 EN+DE chapeaux for 50(3) + 50(4) sub-paragraphs 1+2 + 50(5) use the same Tier-3 mirror after Tier-2 truncation on the orchestrator's fetch. Article 50 summary fields enumerate all four paragraphs' statutory carve-outs verbatim (anti-hand-wave discipline per Day-3 lesson 5: 50(1)+(2)+(3)+(4) all have law-enforcement carve-outs; 50(2) also carries assistive-function carve-out; 50(4) sub-paragraph 1 also carries artistic/satirical carve-out; 50(4) sub-paragraph 2 also carries human-review/editorial-responsibility carve-out). Snapshot test extended to project `article_12`, `article_50`, and `three_category` aggregates (load-bearing fields only — `applicable`, `triggered_by` / `triggered_articles`, `required_articles`, `applicable_categories`, `source` — NOT `items[]`/`title_*`/`disclaimer_*` which are synced website-managed copy per the Day-3 lesson-2 design choice). 77 new unit tests across `test/rules/article-12.spec.ts` (14), `test/rules/article-50.spec.ts` (36), `test/rules/three-category.spec.ts` (17), `test/scripts/sync-three-category.spec.ts` (9, including check-mode + drift-detection tests + fixture-based AST parsing); plus 9 regenerated snapshots covering the 9 fixtures. Total test count 165 → 242. New devDependency: `tsx@^4.22.0` (TypeScript runner for the sync script). New `package.json` scripts: `"sync:three-category"` and `"sync:three-category:check"`.
- **Day 4 — Article 10/13/14/15 high-risk obligation cascade rule modules.** Four pure-function classifiers that cascade off `AnnexIIIResult.high_risk && !suppressed_by_article_5` and emit `Article{N}Result` with `applicable`, `triggered_by` (article_5 flag + annex_iii_domains list), `summary_en`/`summary_de` (verbatim EUR-Lex Article 10/13/14/15 chapeaux), and EUR-Lex `source`. Article 10 applies to all high-risk systems per EUR-Lex Art 10(1) chapeau ("which make use of techniques involving the training of AI models with data") — does NOT narrow to specific Annex III paragraphs, contra the Day-3 handover's hand-wave "applies to Annex III 1+5+6+7" interpretation. Article 12 (record-keeping/logging) is intentionally NOT a Day-4 module — it shows up in `three-category.ts` Day 5 as Cat-2 + Cat-3 pairing. All four modules consume `(AnnexIIIResult, Article5Result)` and never duplicate the high-risk decision; they project the upstream signal into an applicability + EUR-Lex-cited summary. EUR-Lex EN+DE wording sourced via Tier-2 EU AI Office Service Desk (`/en/ai-act/article-{10,13,14,15}` + `/de/ai-act/article-{10,13,14,15}`) — Tier-1 EUR-Lex HTML returned empty on programmatic fetch as of 2026-05-15 (same JS-shell rendering issue seen Day 3); Tier-1 PDF remains the canonical primary source. No Tier-3 cross-validation needed (Tier-2 DE coverage complete for these four articles). 1 new Day-4 fixture (`test/fixtures/use-cases/day4/01-low-risk-en.json`) exercises the non-high-risk path (low-risk office-cleaning-scheduler — distinct from the 8 Day-3 fixtures which cover prohibition + 7 high-risk paths). Snapshot test extended to project Article-10/13/14/15 results across all 8 Day-3 + 1 Day-4 fixtures (load-bearing fields only — `applicable`, `triggered_by`, `source` — per the Day-3 lesson-2 design choice). 52 new unit tests across `test/rules/article-{10,13,14,15}.spec.ts` (13 each) + 5 new snapshot-spec assertions; total test count 108 → 165.
- **Day 3 — Article 5 prohibited-practices + Article 6 / Annex III high-risk classification rule modules with EN+DE disambiguation, scope-qualifier handling, and 8-fixture snapshot test suite.** New `src/rules/article-5.ts` (8 letters a-h with the Art 5(1)(d) "solely on profiling" disambiguator), `src/rules/article-6-annex-iii.ts` (8 high-risk domains with sub-letter narrowing for domains 1, 4, 5, 6 and Annex III.5(c) life/health insurance scope rule), `src/rules/index.ts` barrel, and `src/data/annex-iii.json` (EUR-Lex-cited Annex III data for all 8 domains with EN+DE summaries). Article 5 prohibition supersedes Annex III high-risk in `classifyAnnexIII()`. `internal_use` scope qualifier never suppresses; `research_only` Art 2(8) carve-out is blocked when "real-world conditions" / "Realbedingungen" phrasing is present. 8 bilingual test fixtures at `test/fixtures/use-cases/day3/` (5 EN + 3 DE), 32 unit tests in `test/rules/article-5.spec.ts` + `test/rules/article-6-annex-iii.spec.ts`, plus 16 snapshot assertions in `test/rules/snapshots.spec.ts`.
- **Day 2 — keyword extractor + EN+DE seed lexicons:** deterministic feature extractor (`src/extract/normalize.ts`, `lang.ts`, `keyword.ts`) with NFKC normalization, EN+DE punctuation handling, hyphen-split for German compound nouns, runtime-discovered lexicon groups for v0.2+ extensibility. EN + DE seed lexicons (`src/data/patterns.{en,de}.json`) covering 8 Annex III categories + 8 Article 5 prohibited categories + 4 Article 50 transparency categories + 3 scope qualifiers (~180 phrases total) with `_meta.notice` flagging the regulator-validator verification gate. 43 unit tests across `test/extract/`. Packaging hardened: `build` script copies `src/data/` into `dist/` so the published npm tarball ships the lexicons.
- **Day 1 scaffold:** repo + `package.json` + `tsconfig.json` (Lucairn @lucairn/* TypeScript conventions), MIT license + CC-BY-4.0 dataset license, vitest config, GitHub Actions CI matrix (Node 18/20/22 × Ubuntu/macOS), README EN + DE with disclaimer + scope blocks, `src/cli.ts` + `src/classify.ts` + `src/index.ts` stubs, `test/classify.spec.ts` smoke covering empty-input and not-yet-implemented guards.
